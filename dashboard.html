About Nalerigu

Nalerigu is the capital of the East Mamprusi Municipal Assembly in the North East Region of Ghana.

It is a vibrant town with a rich cultural heritage and a growing economy.

Northern part of Ghana

Nalerigu is located in the North East Region of Ghana, approximately 650 kilometers north of Accra.

It is situated in the Guinea Savannah ecological zone and is known for its scenic landscapes and diverse wildlife.

Culture

Nalerigu is home to the Mamprusi people, who have a rich cultural heritage.

The town is known for its vibrant festivals, traditional music, and local cuisine.

Tourism

Nalerigu offers a range of tourist attractions, including the Nalerigu Market, the East Mamprusi Museum, and the Gambaga Scarp.

Visitors can experience the local culture, try traditional foods, and explore the scenic landscapes.

Discover Science!

A Fun Book for Young Scientists

Plants: Nature's Helpers

Plants are living things that grow in soil. They use sunlight, water, and air to make their own food in a process called photosynthesis.

Plants have roots, stems, leaves, and sometimes flowers. Roots hold the plant in the ground and take in water.

Fun Fact: Some plants can eat insects!

Animals: Our Friends

Animals are living things that move and eat food. They can be big like elephants or small like ants.

Animals need food, water, and air to live. Some animals, like birds, can fly, while others, like fish, swim in water.

Fun Fact: A cheetah is the fastest land animal!

The Water Cycle

Water moves around the Earth in a cycle. It starts in rivers and oceans, then goes to the sky and back again.

The steps are: evaporation (water turns into vapor), condensation (vapor turns into clouds), precipitation (rain or snow falls), and collection (water gathers back in rivers and oceans).

Fun Fact: Raindrops can fall as fast as 20 miles per hour!

Weather and Seasons

Weather is what the air is like outside, like sunny, rainy, or windy. Seasons change how the weather feels throughout the year.

There are four seasons: spring, summer, autumn, and winter. Each season has different weather and temperatures.

Fun Fact: A rainbow appears when sunlight shines through raindrops!

Keep exploring the world of science! Ask questions and have fun learning!

Chapter 1: The Forest Path

Once upon a time, there was a boy named Salifu. He lived in a small village surrounded by tall trees. One sunny morning, Salifu decided to explore the forest near his home.

As he walked, he heard birds singing and saw squirrels chasing each other. The path was covered with soft green moss. Salifu felt happy and curious. "What will I find today?" he wondered.

Suddenly, he saw a shiny blue stone on the ground. It sparkled in the sunlight. Salifu picked it up and felt a warm tingle in his hand. "This is no ordinary stone," he said to himself.

Chapter 2: Meeting Sasabosa

Salifu kept walking, holding the blue stone tightly. The forest grew quieter, and the air felt magical. Then, he heard a soft giggle. "Who's there?" Eli called out.

From behind a big tree, a creature appeared. It was fluffy, with bright green fur and big round eyes. "Hello! I'm Sasabosa!" it said, bouncing with excitement. Salifu smiled. "I'm Salifu. What's a Sasabosa?"

Sasabosa laughed. "I'm a friend of the forest! That stone you found is special. It brought you to me. Want to see something amazing?" Salifu nodded eagerly.

Chapter 3: The Hidden Valley

Sasabosa led Salifu through a secret tunnel under a giant tree. They came out in a hidden valley filled with colorful flowers and glowing butterflies. "Welcome to my home!" said Sasabosa.

Eli saw tiny houses made of leaves and a sparkling river. Sasabosa showed him how to make a flower crown. They played and laughed all day. "This is the best adventure ever!" Salifu said.

As the sun set, Sasabosa gave Salifu a small green leaf. "Keep this to remember me," it said. Salifu promised to visit again. He waved goodbye and walked home, his heart full of joy.

The End

Fun Activities

Activity 1: Draw Sasabosa

Draw a picture of Sasabosa. What color is its fur? What kind of eyes does it have? Use your imagination!

Activity 2: Write a Letter

Write a letter to Sasabosa. Tell it about your favorite part of the story or what you would do in the hidden valley.

Activity 3: Story Continuation

Write a short story about Salifu's next visit to Sasabosa. What new adventure do they have?
